# Meta-Learned Models of Cognition

**Binz, Dasgupta, Jagadish, Botvinick, Wang & Schulz** — *Behavioral and Brain Sciences* (2024).
arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.06729 · BBS: https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/behavioral-and-brain-sciences/article/abs/metalearned-models-of-cognition/F95059E07AE6E82AE56C4164A5384A18

**Why it's in the course — claim (b):** learn the *learning algorithm* (and its inductive
biases) by meta-training across a task distribution, instead of hand-designing it. The
constructive answer to the representation gap that Niv (claim (a)) exposes and the
structured-priors thesis of *Building Machines That Learn and Think Like People* argues over.

Lead group: **Eric Schulz** (Helmholtz Munich, Institute for Human-Centered AI; formerly
MPI for Biological Cybernetics, Tübingen), with DeepMind co-authors (Botvinick, Wang, Dasgupta).
